PRADHAN MANTRI AWAS YOJANA (PMAY) — CREDIT LINKED SUBSIDY SCHEME (CLSS)
HDFC INTERNAL REFERENCE GUIDE — SYNTHETIC DATA
=======================================================================

1. SCHEME OVERVIEW
   PMAY-CLSS provides interest subsidy on home loans for purchase/construction of house.
   Objective: Housing for All — affordable housing for economically weaker sections and middle-income groups.

2. INCOME CATEGORIES & SUBSIDY

   | Category | Annual Income (INR) | Max Loan for Subsidy | Interest Subsidy | Max Carpet Area | Net Present Value (NPV) |
   |----------|--------------------:|---------------------:|-----------------:|----------------:|------------------------:|
   | EWS      |     Up to 3,00,000  |          6,00,000    |        6.50%     |     30 sq m     |          Up to 2,67,280 |
   | LIG      | 3,00,001-6,00,000   |          6,00,000    |        6.50%     |     60 sq m     |          Up to 2,67,280 |
   | MIG-I    | 6,00,001-12,00,000  |          9,00,000    |        4.00%     |    160 sq m     |          Up to 2,35,068 |
   | MIG-II   |12,00,001-18,00,000  |         12,00,000    |        3.00%     |    200 sq m     |          Up to 2,30,156 |

3. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
   a) Beneficiary family must not own a pucca house in any part of India
   b) Beneficiary family must not have availed central government housing scheme benefit
   c) Family defined as: Husband, wife, unmarried sons, unmarried daughters
   d) Adult earning members (even if married) treated as separate household for MIG
   e) Women ownership/co-ownership mandatory for EWS/LIG
   f) Aadhaar mandatory for all beneficiaries

4. DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED
   - Self-declaration of income (for EWS/LIG) OR ITR (for MIG)
   - Aadhaar card of all family members
   - PAN card
   - Self-certification: "No pucca house in India" affidavit
   - Property documents showing carpet area compliance
   - Bank account details for subsidy credit

5. HDFC OPERATIONAL PROCESS
   Step 1: Customer applies for home loan with PMAY request
   Step 2: Branch verifies eligibility (income, first-time buyer, carpet area)
   Step 3: Loan sanctioned at regular home loan rate
   Step 4: PMAY claim submitted to NHB via HDFC corporate office
   Step 5: NHB verifies and releases subsidy to HDFC
   Step 6: Subsidy credited to customer's loan account (reduces principal)
   Step 7: EMI recalculated post-subsidy credit

6. IMPORTANT NOTES FOR SALES TEAM
   - PMAY is a POWERFUL sales tool — subsidy effectively reduces the interest rate by 2-6%
   - For a INR 30L loan at 8.5%, PMAY MIG-I subsidy saves the customer approximately INR 5.4L over tenure
   - Many customers are UNAWARE of their eligibility — proactively check for every first-time buyer
   - Subsidy is credited as lump sum principal reduction — EMI drops immediately
   - PMAY benefit can be combined with other HDFC schemes (festive offers, women concession)

7. COMMON REJECTION REASONS
   - Family already owns a pucca house
   - Carpet area exceeds category limit
   - Income exceeds category limit
   - Not a first-time buyer
   - Property not RERA registered
   - Incomplete documentation

8. SUBSIDY CALCULATION EXAMPLES

   Below are three worked-out examples illustrating actual EMI savings under each PMAY category.
   All calculations assume reducing-balance EMI method.

   EXAMPLE A — EWS Category (Annual Income: INR 2,40,000)
   -------------------------------------------------------
   Customer: Mr. Ramesh Kumar, daily-wage worker, Indore
   Property: 1 BHK flat, 28 sq m carpet area, RERA-approved builder
   Property Cost: INR 9,00,000
   Loan Amount: INR 8,10,000 (90% LTV)
   Loan Tenure: 20 years (240 months)
   Interest Rate: 6.75% p.a.

   WITHOUT PMAY Subsidy:
     Monthly EMI = INR 6,149
     Total Interest over 20 years = INR 6,65,760
     Total Amount Paid = INR 14,75,760

   PMAY Subsidy Calculation:
     Eligible loan for subsidy = INR 6,00,000 (capped)
     Interest subsidy rate = 6.50%
     NPV of subsidy (at 9% discount rate over 20 years) = INR 2,67,280
     Subsidy credited to loan account = INR 2,67,280
     Effective principal after subsidy = INR 8,10,000 - 2,67,280 = INR 5,42,720

   WITH PMAY Subsidy:
     Revised Monthly EMI = INR 4,122
     Revised Total Interest = INR 4,46,408
     Revised Total Amount Paid = INR 9,89,128

   NET SAVINGS:
     EMI Reduction = INR 6,149 - INR 4,122 = INR 2,027 per month
     Total Interest Savings = INR 2,19,352
     Total Effective Saving (including principal reduction) = INR 4,86,632
     Effective Interest Rate felt by customer ≈ 3.80% p.a.

9. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)

   Q1: Can a customer who already owns a plot of land (without any structure) apply for PMAY?
   A1: Yes. Owning a vacant plot is NOT considered owning a pucca house. The customer is eligible
       as long as they do not own a pucca house anywhere in India. They can apply for PMAY for
       construction on their existing plot.

   Q2: What if the customer's spouse already owns a house in another city?
   A2: The beneficiary FAMILY (husband + wife + unmarried children) must not own a pucca house
       anywhere in India. If the spouse owns a house, the family is NOT eligible for EWS/LIG.
       However, for MIG-I and MIG-II, an adult earning member can be treated as a separate
       household and may still qualify independently.

   Q3: Can PMAY subsidy be combined with HDFC's festive rate concession or women borrower discount?
   A3: Yes. PMAY subsidy is a government benefit that reduces principal, while HDFC concessions
       reduce the interest rate. Both can be availed simultaneously. This makes the effective
       rate even lower — a strong selling point.

   Q4: How long does it take for the subsidy to be credited to the customer's loan account?
   A4: Typically 3-6 months from the date of loan disbursement. The process involves:
       (a) HDFC submits claim to NHB within 30 days of disbursement
       (b) NHB verification takes 45-90 days
       (c) NHB releases subsidy to HDFC
       (d) HDFC credits to customer within 7 working days of receipt
       RMs should set expectations with the customer upfront about this timeline.

   Q5: What happens if the PMAY claim is rejected by NHB?
   A5: The loan continues at the originally sanctioned rate. There is no penalty to the customer.
       Common rejection reasons include Aadhaar mismatch, duplicate subsidy claim (already availed
       under another lender), or income/carpet area non-compliance. The branch can resubmit with
       corrected documents within 30 days of rejection.

   Q6: Can an NRI apply for PMAY?
   A6: No. PMAY-CLSS is available only to Indian residents. The beneficiary must have an Indian
       residential address and valid Aadhaar. NRIs should be guided to the regular NRI Home Loan
       product (HL-NRI) which has its own competitive features.

   Q7: Is there a minimum loan amount required for PMAY subsidy?
   A7: No minimum loan amount is prescribed by the government. However, HDFC's minimum home loan
       amount is INR 3 lakhs (for PMAY product). The subsidy is calculated on the eligible loan
       amount (up to the cap for each category), regardless of the total loan amount.

   Q8: Can a customer who bought a resale flat (secondary market) avail PMAY?
   A8: Yes, for EWS and LIG categories, both new construction and resale (existing) properties
       are eligible, provided the carpet area is within limits. For MIG-I and MIG-II, only
       NEW construction (including builder flats under construction) is eligible. The property
       must have a valid sale deed and the carpet area must be independently verified.

   Q9: What if the customer's income is borderline (e.g., INR 5.8L — close to LIG limit)?
   A9: Income is assessed based on the self-declaration (for EWS/LIG) or the latest ITR
       (for MIG). The income of ALL earning members in the family is clubbed for EWS/LIG.
       If borderline, counsel the customer to provide accurate documentation — inflating or
       deflating income for PMAY eligibility is a criminal offense. If income rises above the
       limit before claim submission, the customer must inform HDFC immediately.

   Q10: Can PMAY be availed for a second home loan if the first was for a different purpose (e.g., plot)?
   A10: No. PMAY-CLSS benefit can only be availed ONCE per beneficiary family. If a family has
        previously received CLSS benefit under any bank/HFC/government scheme, they cannot
        avail it again, even if the earlier property was sold or the loan was closed. The NHB
        system maintains a central registry (CLAP portal) that prevents duplicate claims.
