    The borrower may be eligible for the following tax benefits under the Income Tax Act, 1961.
    These are indicative and subject to prevailing tax laws. Borrowers are advised to consult
    their tax advisor for personalized guidance.

    A) Section 80C — Principal Repayment Deduction
       - Deduction on principal component of EMI paid during the financial year
       - Maximum deduction: INR 1,50,000 per annum (shared with other 80C investments)
       - Applicable from the year of possession / completion of construction
       - Stamp duty and registration charges paid are also eligible under Section 80C
         (in the year of payment only)
       - Condition: The property must not be sold within 5 years of possession, otherwise
         the deduction claimed shall be reversed in the year of sale

    B) Section 24(b) — Interest on Housing Loan
       - Deduction on interest component of EMI paid during the financial year
       - Self-occupied property: Maximum INR 2,00,000 per annum
         (provided construction is completed within 5 years from the end of the FY in which
         the loan was taken)
       - Let-out / deemed let-out property: Entire interest amount is deductible (no upper limit)
       - Pre-construction interest: Interest paid during the construction period can be claimed
         in 5 equal installments starting from the year of completion
       - If construction exceeds 5 years, the maximum deduction reduces to INR 30,000 per annum

    C) Section 80EE / Section 80EEA — Additional Interest Deduction for First-Time Buyers
       - Section 80EE (applicable for loans sanctioned between 01-Apr-2016 and 31-Mar-2017):
         Additional deduction of up to INR 50,000 on interest, over and above Section 24(b)
         Conditions: Loan amount ≤ INR 35,00,000; property value ≤ INR 50,00,000;
         borrower should not own any other house property on the date of loan sanction
       - Section 80EEA (applicable for loans sanctioned between 01-Apr-2019 and 31-Mar-2022):
         Additional deduction of up to INR 1,50,000 on interest
         Conditions: Stamp duty value of property ≤ INR 45,00,000;
         borrower should not own any other residential property on the date of loan sanction
       - Note: Sections 80EE and 80EEA are not simultaneously available. Applicability depends
         on the date of loan sanction.
